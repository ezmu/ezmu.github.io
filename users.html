📱 An Open Letter to the End User

(An Apology from the Software Industry)
Emotional title: "Forgive us… Your engineers have betrayed you."
Practical title: "Why is your new phone so slow? (Because we were lazy.)"
“Dear user… yes, you, reading this on a hot screen with a dying battery.
This is a letter you never expected, and perhaps never wanted. But we owe it to you.
We, the software industry, apologize to you.”

⚰️ What are we apologizing for?

1. We apologize because your new phone is slow.

You bought a $1000 phone. Faster processor, faster RAM, sharper screen. But when you opened our app… it froze. A loading bar appeared. Then a skeleton loader. Then another bar. Then the page loaded after 5 seconds.

This is not your phone's fault. This is our code's fault. We thought "fast hardware" excused our laziness. We were wrong. Fast hardware exposed our laziness; it did not hide it.

2. We apologize because your battery dies in 4 hours.

Theoretically, your phone could run 12 hours. Practically, our app – bloated with JavaScript, ads, and trackers – devours your battery like termites. We know every line of code consumes power. But we added it anyway. Because we "were in a hurry." Because "the feature was requested." Because "performance is a problem for future us."

Now future us is here, and you're searching for a charger.

pie title "Where Does Your Battery Go?" "Actual app functionality (10%)" : 10 "Tracking & Ads (40%)" : 40 "Unused JavaScript (30%)" : 30 "Inefficient rendering (20%)" : 20

3. We apologize because your data plan ran out early.

Our app decided to load a 4K video on a 6-inch screen, even though your eye cannot see the difference. It decided to auto-play, even if you were in another room. It decided to reload the page every time you scrolled.

All these decisions were made by a lazy engineer, but YOU paid the data bill.

4. We apologize because you feel your phone is "obsolete" after one year.

Ads convince you that you need the latest processor, the fastest RAM, a 120Hz screen. The truth? If we wrote code with 1999 efficiency, your 2018 phone would fly.

We collude with phone manufacturers. They sell you new devices, and we write code that doesn't work on old ones. A successful partnership… and YOU are the loser.

5. We apologize because we excluded you if you are poor (or live in a remote place).

If you own a phone from 2018, or internet below 5Mbps, or don't speak English… you are invisible to us. We build apps on the latest MacBook, the fastest fiber, in the designer's language. We test on our dream devices, not on your reality.

The result: You stand outside the digital door, hearing noise of the "digital revolution" that doesn't work on your device. This is not a fault of your circumstances. This is digital colonialism. And we are the colonizers.

pie title "Who Can Actually Use Your 'Modern' App?" "People with new devices + fast internet (30%)" : 30 "People you excluded (70%)" : 70

💀 What could have been? (If we had been honest)

If engineers learned fundamentals instead of just "frameworks."
If product managers said "no" to unnecessary features.
If executives measured "technical debt" like they measure "profit."

Then your app would:

  • Load in 0.5 seconds (not 5 seconds).
  • Consume 70% less battery.
  • Work on any phone made after 2015.
  • Not discriminate between rich and poor.
  • Not burn your planet.

But we chose the easy path.

🕯️ Now… what will we do? (Because an apology without change is just begging)

  • Spread awareness: We will tell our arrogant colleagues that the user is not stupid. They notice the slowness.
  • Hold ourselves accountable: We will add a "Performance Budget." Any feature that slows the app will not be released.
  • Test on old devices: We will buy a 2018 phone and test on it before every release.
  • Simplify code: We will write simple functions instead of importing 1000 packages.
“Don't applaud us now. Watch us in 6 months.”

📜 The bottom line

Dear user…

  • You are not stupid. Apps really are slow.
  • Your phone is not obsolete. Our code is obsolete.
  • Your data plan is not small. Our app is the glutton.
  • The planet is not doomed. We doomed it by burning.

We owe you an apology. But more importantly… we owe you change.

So either we correct our path, or you continue paying the bill (with your money, your time, your planet).

The decision is ours, the software industry.

But remember… you, the user, have only one finger. With it, you decide: open the slow app, or look for an alternative.

“Use your power. Do not accept slowness. Do not accept drain. Do not accept exclusion.
Only when companies feel your anger, will software improve.”
Signed,
The Software Industry (trying to repent)

And you… will you forgive us?
